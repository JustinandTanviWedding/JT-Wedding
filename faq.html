Your passport must be valid for at least 6 months beyond your travel dates — this is required for entry into India. We recommend checking your expiration date as soon as possible to avoid any last-minute stress.

Renewal timeline:
· If your passport expires within 12 months of your travel, begin renewal soon
· Standard processing: ~6–8 weeks
· Expedited processing: ~2–3 weeks

You can apply or renew at travel.state.gov.
Yes — US citizens require a visa to enter India. The easiest option is the Indian e-Visa, which can be applied for entirely online.

Key details:
· Apply at indianvisaonline.gov.in (the official Indian government site)
· Processing typically takes 3–5 business days — but apply at least 2–4 weeks before travel to be safe
· The tourist e-Visa is valid for 1 year from the date of issue and allows multiple entries
· Cost is approximately $25–$80 depending on duration selected

Do not use third-party visa sites — the official government site above is the correct and only place to apply.

Attire & Dress Code
Haldi
Wear yellow or orange — this is the one time getting messy is part of the fun. Turmeric paste can stain, so avoid anything you'd be sad to ruin. Light cotton kurtas, simple salwar suits, or any comfortable yellow outfit work perfectly. Turmeric is considered sacred and purifying, believed to bless, cleanse, and beautify the couple before the wedding.

Mehendi
Colourful and festive — think greens, pinks, turquoise, and bright prints. This is a relaxed, joyful event, so opt for something comfortable and vibrant.

Sangeet
This is the party night — bold, festive, and fun. Lehengas, anarkalis, sharara sets, or dressy Indo-Western outfits for women. Men can wear a kurta with trousers or a blazer with Indian elements. Bright, celebratory colours encouraged — this is your chance to go all out.
